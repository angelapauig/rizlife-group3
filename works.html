Historical Context

Spanish Colonial Rule

The Philippines was under Spanish rule for over 300 years, during which the native population suffered from oppressive policies, forced labor, high taxes, and limited political representation. The Catholic Church played a significant role in maintaining control over the population, often collaborating with the colonial government.

Propaganda Movement

Rizal wrote this essay as part of the Propaganda Movement, a group of Filipino expatriates in Europe who sought to advocate for reforms in the Philippines. The movement aimed to achieve equal rights for Filipinos, representation in the Spanish Cortes, and the secularization of the clergy.

The Philippines A Century Hence

The Plot Summary


Part 1

Rizal begins by analyzing the past and present conditions of the Philippines under Spanish rule. He describes how the Filipino people lost their traditions, culture, and sense of identity due to colonization. The Spanish colonial system, supported by the Catholic Church, suppressed the native population and prevented them from progressing.


Part 2

Rizal discusses the ethical and moral degradation of the Filipino people under colonial rule. He argues that the Spanish colonial government and the Church have systematically oppressed the Filipinos, leading to a loss of self-respect and dignity. However, he also notes that the Filipino spirit is resilient and that the people are beginning to awaken to their plight.


Part 3

Rizal predicts the future of the Philippines, suggesting that if Spain does not implement meaningful reforms, the Philippines will eventually seek independence. He warns that continued oppression will lead to a violent uprising, as the Filipino people will no longer tolerate injustice.



Part 4

Rizal concludes by emphasizing the importance of education, good governance, and self-determination. He argues that the Philippines must either be fully integrated into Spain as an equal partner or be granted independence. He warns that if Spain continues to exploit the Philippines, it will lose the colony forever.
