Introduction

"The Philippines a Century Hence" is one of Jose Rizal’s most profound essays, written between 1889 and 1890. In this work, Rizal predicts the future of the Philippines under Spanish colonial rule, analyzing the past, present, and potential future of the country. He emphasizes the need for reforms, self-determination, and good governance, warning of the consequences of continued oppression and exploitation by colonial powers.

Rizal’s essay was published in La Solidaridad, the newspaper of the Propaganda Movement, which sought to advocate for reforms in the Philippines under Spanish rule. The essay reflects Rizal’s deep concern for his homeland and his desire to awaken a sense of nationalism among Filipinos.

Bibliography


José Rizal (1861–1896) was a Filipino nationalist, writer, and revolutionary. Born in Calamba, Laguna, he was a doctor, linguist, and reformist who fought for peaceful reforms under Spanish rule.


His novels, "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," exposed colonial injustices, inspiring the Philippine Revolution. Arrested for alleged rebellion, he was executed on December 30, 1896, in Bagumbayan (now Luneta Park). His martyrdom made him the Philippine national hero and a symbol of freedom.
